# Competitor Analysis: Small Business SaaS for AU/NZ Local Government

## 1. Our Community / SmartyGrants (ourcommunity.com.au / smartygrants.com.au)

**Company Profile:** Australian social business founded in 1999, providing technology solutions for not-for-profits, charities, and funders including local government councils.

**Product Suite:**
- SmartyGrants (grant management platform)
- SmartySearch (grants search tool for councils)
- SmartyFile (data storage and sharing)
- Funding Centre (grants database)
- GiveNow (donations platform)
- Institute of Community Directors (governance training)

**Target Market:** 830+ funders across Australia including federal, state, and local government (City of Melbourne, City of Sydney, City of Perth, Brisbane City Council, etc.)

**Key Statistics:**
- $50 billion+ in grants administered
- 830+ funders using the platform
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certified

**Conversion Strategies:**

The site leads with a strong localization message: "Built in Australia for Australian grantmakers" - establishing trust with government buyers who prefer local vendors. The primary CTA "Try SmartyGrants" appears twice above the fold, encouraging low-commitment trial engagement.

Social proof is displayed through an extensive logo wall organized by sector (Federal Government, State Government, Local Government, Philanthropic, Corporate, NFP) making it easy for prospects to find peers. Major city councils are prominently featured.

The "Featured Resources" carousel showcases thought leadership content (whitepapers on grant standardisation, outcomes-oriented grantmaking, risk management) positioning them as industry experts rather than just software vendors.

A news section with recent articles demonstrates ongoing engagement with the sector and keeps the site fresh. Testimonials use full quotes with specific praise about customer service and responsiveness.

The navigation includes "Institute of Grants Management" and "Training" - showing they offer more than just software, building deeper relationships with customers.


## 2. Symphony3 (symphony3.com)

**Company Profile:** Australian company founded in 2011, specializing in local government data integration and digital transformation solutions.

**Product Suite:**
- SmartGlue (integration platform for local government systems)
- OpenCities (website and digital experience platform)
- Smart Forms
- Custom Dashboards (e.g., Emergency Dashboard)
- AI Agents integration

**Target Market:** Local government councils across Australia (operating since 2011)

**Key Statistics:**
- 65,000 hours saved for clients
- $3.2 million dollars saved
- 100,000 cumulative transactions

**Integration Partners:** Drupal, eWay, Granicus, Amazon Connect, Datascape, SharePoint, Civica, Solr, CouncilWise, Greenlight, Xero

**Conversion Strategies:**

Symphony3 leads with a clear, benefit-focused value proposition: "We connect, automate, monitor and secure your Local Government dataflows." This immediately communicates what they do and for whom.

The hero section features a professional woman using a tablet - representing the modern council worker they serve. The primary CTA "Book a demo" appears prominently in both the header and hero section.

Quantified impact metrics are displayed prominently: "65,000 Hours saved," "$3.2 Million Dollars saved," and "100,000 Cumulative transactions" - providing concrete ROI evidence that appeals to budget-conscious council decision-makers.

The integration partner logos (Civica, Datascape, CouncilWise, etc.) demonstrate compatibility with existing council systems - addressing a key concern for IT procurement.

A "Community" section and regular webinars (AI Pollinator series) position them as thought leaders and create ongoing engagement opportunities. The blog features council-specific case studies (City of Greater Bendigo Emergency Dashboard).

An email newsletter signup captures leads who aren't ready to book a demo, with the promise of "latest insights on digital strategy and transformation."


## 3. Pulse Software (pulsesoftware.com)

**Company Profile:** Australian software company that began supporting local councils and expanded to serve public sector, not-for-profit, and private sector organizations.

**Product Suite:**
- Talent Management modules
- Governance modules
- Corporate Planning & Reporting
- Employee Performance Management
- Risk Management
- Strategy alignment tools

**Target Market:** Public Sector (local councils), Not-for-Profit, and Private Sector organizations across Australia

**Council Clients Include:** Ballina Shire Council, Campbelltown City Council, City of Boroondara, Port Macquarie Hastings Council, Orange City Council

**Conversion Strategies:**

Pulse Software positions itself with a clear value proposition: "Redefining HR Software - Combining Talent Management, Governance and Strategy." This differentiates them from pure HR or pure governance tools.

The homepage features an interactive dashboard mockup showing personalized user experience ("Hi, Robert") with tasks, goals tracking (70% completed), and quick links - demonstrating the product's usability without requiring a demo.

Key platform differentiators are highlighted with icons: Intuitive Interface, Modular Solutions, Cloud-based (SaaS), Robust Reporting, Flexible Configuration, and Industry Configurability. This addresses common procurement concerns upfront.

The "Sectors" page explicitly mentions their origin story: "Pulse Software began its journey supporting local councils" - establishing credibility with government buyers by showing deep domain expertise.

Testimonials from named council roles (Business Systems Analyst at Ballina Shire, Executive Manager at Campbelltown City Council, Coordinator at City of Boroondara) provide specific, relatable social proof with outcome-focused quotes like "What used to take weeks... now only takes hours."

Primary CTAs are "Get in touch" and "Request a demo" - a consultative approach appropriate for enterprise sales to councils.
